# Spirr — Outreach assets (copy-paste ready)

Companion to `USER-TESTING-PLAN.md`. Ready-to-send copy for getting the first testers, NO + EN.

**How to use these — the three rules that actually matter:**
1. **Warm before cold.** Send Asset 1 to gardening friends/family/neighbours *first*. Five warm contacts beat 500 cold impressions.
2. **Contribute before you promote.** In FB groups, be the person who answers frost/timing questions (Asset 2). Never drop a cold "test my app" link — most Norwegian garden groups ban it.
3. **One question, not a survey.** The ask is always *"does this date look right for your garden?"* — a 60-second yes, not a season-long commitment.

Replace `[LENKE]` / `[LINK]` with your deployed URL, and `[dato]`/`[sted]` with the real values.

## Asset 1 — The one-question message (warm contact)

*Summer/harvest version — zero setup. The welcome screen has a postnummer field that instantly shows what perennials/berries are in season at their location, no garden needed. This is the frictionless share for June–September.*

**Norsk:**
> Hei! Du som har hage 🌱 — jeg har laga et lite verktøy som viser hva som er klart å høste akkurat nå der du bor, ut fra værstasjonen og høyden din (ekte lokale datoer, ikke generelle råd).
>
> Kan du gjøre meg én tjeneste? Åpne denne og skriv inn postnummeret ditt — du ser med én gang hva som er i sesong. Og si meg: **stemmer det med bæra/frukta i din egen hage?**
>
> [LENKE]
>
> Ingen innlogging, ingenting å sette opp — det bare virker. Tar 30 sekunder.

**English:**
> Hey! Since you've got a garden 🌱 — I built a little tool that shows what's ready to harvest right now where you live, based on your weather station and elevation (real local dates, not generic advice).
>
> Could you do me one favour? Open this and type in your postcode — you'll instantly see what's in season. And tell me: **does it match the berries/fruit in your own garden?**
>
> [LINK]
>
> No sign-up, nothing to set up — it just works. Takes 30 seconds.

*Spring version (Feb–May): swap "klart å høste" → "hva du bør så nå" and the ask → "stemmer så-datoene med hagen din?" The same postnummer field drives the "Hva passer å så nå?" card.*

## Asset 2 — The "be the frost-date answerer" group comment

Use when someone asks *"når kan jeg så [X] i [sted]?"* Lead with genuine help; the tool is a soft, only-if-they-want-it offer at the end. Mod-safe.

**Norsk:**
> For [sted] ligger siste vårfrost normalt rundt **[dato]** (værnormaler 1991–2020 for stasjonen nærmest deg). Så [X] kan du regne med å [så ute / plante ut] rundt **[dato]**. Bor du høyere enn sentrum, legg på noen dager — det blir fort en uke per 100 høydemeter.
>
> (Jeg har forresten laga et gratis verktøy som regner ut akkurat dette for postnummeret ditt — si fra om du vil ha lenken 🙂)

**English:**
> For [place], the last spring frost is normally around **[date]** (1991–2020 normals for your nearest station). So you can expect to [sow / plant out] [X] around **[date]**. If you're higher up than the town centre, add a few days — it works out to roughly a week per 100 m of elevation.
>
> (I actually built a free tool that works this out for your exact postcode — happy to share the link if useful 🙂)

## Asset 4 — Local hooks (Sogndal / Vestland — surprising, specific, shareable)

Numbers are what Spirr actually computes for postnummer 6856. A specific local fact travels; "my garden app" doesn't. Use as a post opener, a comment, or a caption.

**Norsk:**
> 🏔️ Visste du at siste vårfrost i **Sogndal sentrum** er nesten **tre uker tidligere** enn oppe i dalen? Ved fjorden (~5 moh) rundt **1. mai** — på 300 moh rundt **21. mai**, og på Anestølen (443 moh) helt til **slutten av mai**. Derfor funker generelle sålister så dårlig her.

> 📍 De fleste hage-apper bruker nærmeste **flyplass**-værstasjon. For Sogndal betyr det 497 moh — men dalbunnen der folk faktisk dyrker ligger på 170. Det er nesten en måneds forskjell i vekstsesong. Spirr bruker dalstasjonen.

> 📏 Frostdatoen din avhenger ikke bare av *hvor i landet* du bor, men *hvor høyt*. Hver 100 høydemeter ≈ en uke senere vårfrost. Spirr regner inn både postnummer og høyde.

**English:**
> 🏔️ Did you know the last spring frost in **Sogndal town centre** is nearly **three weeks earlier** than up the valley? By the fjord (~5 m) around **1 May** — at 300 m around **21 May**, and at Anestølen (443 m) into **late May**. That's why generic sowing lists work so badly here.

> 📍 Most garden apps use the nearest **airport** weather station. For Sogndal that's 497 m — but the valley floor where people actually grow is at 170 m. That's nearly a month's difference in growing season. Spirr uses the valley station.

> 📏 Your frost date depends not just on *where in the country* you live, but *how high*. Every 100 m of elevation ≈ a week later last frost. Spirr accounts for both postcode and elevation.
